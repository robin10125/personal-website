NN Microscope

An educational neural network visualizer for stepping through forward passes, backpropagation, gradients, and optimizer updates.

NN Microscope is a browser-based teaching tool for making neural network training less abstract. It lets a learner build a small network, step through the forward pass, inspect tensors and formulas, then walk backward through the same computation graph to see where each gradient comes from.

The motivation was to turn the parts of training that are usually hidden inside frameworks into something visible and concrete. The program shows dense networks, transformer blocks, convolution concepts, loss curves, decision boundaries, parameter updates, and numerical gradient checks.

It is mainly used for education: learning backpropagation, understanding optimizer updates, and building intuition for how neural networks change during training. The program was made with Claude.
